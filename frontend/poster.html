Context & Challenges

The energy transition demands complex grid management to integrate intermittent renewables and optimize costs at national scale. This simulator models France's grid to address three core challenges:

Balancing: Match supply and demand in real-time.
Finance: Minimize production costs via the SPOT market (Merit Order).
Security: Prevent blackouts and manage congestion (N-1 Crash Test).
3
Modeling & Resolution

France's grid is abstracted as a directed graph G = (N, A) via NetworkX. This is a Minimum Cost Flow optimization problem: nodes are cities or plants; arcs are high-voltage lines with capacity & cost constraints.

Min-Cost Flow Algorithm
Busacker–Gowen

Successive Shortest Paths method — iteratively solves the economic dispatch problem step by step.

01
Search for the cheapest augmenting path (Bellman-Ford).
02
Saturate the line along this path up to its capacity.
03
Update the residual graph and repeat until equilibrium.
★ Carbon Tax integrated dynamically — modifies arc costs live to reflect CO₂ pricing in the dispatch.
